### Scope

Served as the sole product partner to [Uchembere Wabwino Maternity Clinic](https://www.uchembere.org) for designing and implementing a digital health learning application. Defined UX/UI and technical requirements for low-literacy and offline environments in Sub-Saharan Africa, enabling onboarding of 30+ non-technical users for concept validation.

### Key Features

- **Optimized for Low-Literacy Users**: Provide a step-by-step, bite-sized series of lessons for Sexual Reproductive Health and Rights (SRHR), accompanied by interactive quizzes and engaging illustrations
- **Offline-First Design**: Requires no internet access after installation, with optional behavioural tracking through Google Analytics and BigQuery for monitoring, evaluation, and personalization
- **AI-Enabled Prototyping**: Built on rapid iterative product cycles using AI-assisted prototyping tools such as Canva AI and Claude Code, ensuring timely delivery and feedback triage to meet the time-sensitive and mission-critical nature of the app
