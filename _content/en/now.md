### Promoting digital participation & inclusion

It's been four months since I strategically moved to Canada's capital, Ottawa, and positioned [my independent business](/services) around consultancy and mentorship for tackling digital inequality. Although things move very slowly here, I started shaping a good sense of local contexts, such as key players, their focus areas, and capacity gaps, thanks to the consistent exposure to various organizations and individuals' messages, as well as my [newsletter](/ab). The immediate goal is to conclude this exploration phase by the end of the month and initiate a more focused project in April.

### Learning French

After moving to Ottawa, where more than 35% of the population is [bilingual](https://www12.statcan.gc.ca/census-recensement/2021/as-sa/fogs-spg/page.cfm?lang=E&topic=6&dguid=2021A00053506008), I suddenly feel obligated to practice French; from business to daily life, available opportunities seem to be noticeably limited if you only speak English. Hence, I'm actively learning the foundation with the help of a [government-funded language class](https://www.canada.ca/en/immigration-refugees-citizenship/services/settle-canada/language-skills/classes.html). Last month, I switched daily journaling from English to French in an audio format, and I'm trying to be able to speak for five consecutive minutes without pausing (currently 2-3 minutes to the best).

### Running for sub-1:30 half marathon

I registered for [Ottawa Half Marathon](https://www.runottawa.ca/races-and-events/half-marathon/) on May 24, 2026. First race in three years! In fact, the most recent race was the one in Ottawa, where I achieved [my current PR of 1:31:20](https://www.notion.so/takuti/Running-0c7b35e5463f4c1e9d368ed1c4000eba?source=copy_link#b0081501ac1a480a96037787f3868535). This time, I aim to beat the record with a sub-1:30 time. Using ChatGPT as a personal coach, I have been working on the prep for the last three months, mostly on a treadmill due to the extreme winter in the city. Now, as spring approaches, I'm excited to bring outdoor runs back to my daily routine.
